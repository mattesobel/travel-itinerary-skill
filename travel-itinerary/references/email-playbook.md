# Vendor Email Playbook

How to draft and track emails to the people who make a trip happen: hotels and concierges, restaurants, drivers, guides, tour operators, and rental hosts. This is one of the most useful things this skill does. A good email gets a fast yes; a vague one starts a slow back-and-forth.

## Principles

- **One clear purpose per email.** A reservation request, a confirmation, or a question. Do not bury three asks in a paragraph.
- **Make it easy to say yes.** Give exact dates, times, party size, and any specifics. The vendor should be able to act without writing back to clarify.
- **Warm and specific, not stiff.** A sentence of genuine enthusiasm ("we're celebrating our anniversary and would love to...") gets better treatment than a form request.
- **Always ask them to confirm.** End with a clear request: "Could you confirm availability, the price per person, and how to pay?"
- **Match their language and time format** where you can. Use 24-hour time for non-US vendors, name the currency, write dates unambiguously (1 Sep 2026, not 9/1).

## Anatomy of a good reservation email

1. **Greeting + who you are** in one line, with the reservation name if there is one.
2. **The ask**, stated plainly: what, exact date, exact time, party size.
3. **Specifics** that matter: a courtyard table, a quiet room, dietary needs, a celebration, an arrival window.
4. **The confirm request**: what you need back (availability, price, payment method, cancellation policy).
5. **A warm close.**

### Template

> Hello {{name}},
>
> I'm {{name}}, {{reservation name / context}}. We'll be visiting {{place}} and would love to {{the ask}}.
>
> - **What:** {{e.g. dinner for two}}
> - **When:** {{exact date}} at {{exact time}}
> - **Party:** {{N people}}
> - **Notes:** {{courtyard table / dietary needs / occasion}}
>
> Could you confirm availability, the price, and how you'd like us to pay or hold the reservation? Happy to share a phone number if helpful.
>
> Thank you so much, we're really looking forward to it.
> {{name}}

## Common email types

- **Hotel pre-arrival:** confirm dates and reservation name, ask about airport transfer options and cost, early check-in, room preference, and anything the concierge can pre-book (dinners, a driver, a spa treatment, a boat).
- **Restaurant reservation:** date, time, party size, seating preference, occasion, dietary restrictions. Ask whether a tasting menu is the only option if that matters.
- **Driver / transfer:** route, date, flight number and landing time, number of bags, child seats, price per route, payment method, where they will meet you.
- **Tour / experience operator:** date, group size, whether it is private or shared, duration, what is included, the weather/seasonal backup plan, deposit and cancellation terms.
- **Reconfirmation (a few days out):** short and friendly, restating the booked details and asking them to confirm the exact pickup time or reservation still stands.

## Tracking in Emails.md

Keep a running log in the trip folder's `Emails.md` (start from `assets/vendor-emails-template.md`), newest at top. For every message:

- Mark it **Sent**, **Received**, or **Draft**.
- Date it and name the vendor and their email.
- Record the **hard facts** in the entry: confirmed times, prices, confirmation numbers, payment method, cancellation policy.
- Flag anything the vendor did **not** address so you know to follow up.

Every time a vendor confirms something, do two things: move the matching item off the brief's **Book-now list**, and record the hard details in the brief's **Lodging / Transfers / Food** sections. The email log and the brief should always agree.

## A note on sending

This skill drafts and tracks. It does not send email on its own unless an email connector is connected and the traveler has asked it to. Default behavior: produce a clean, ready-to-paste draft and let the traveler send it. If an email connector is available and the traveler approves, sending can be automated, but always show the draft first.
